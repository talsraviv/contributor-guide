Lead a Co-Hacking Meetup Group

Passionate about DuckDuckGo? Love hacking with other people? Care about a particular field - and feel it could use better Instant Answer coverage?

Organizing DuckDuckGo Co-Hacking Meetups (Quack & Hacks!) is a really fun way to bring people together to build live, open-source Instant Answers together.

See our current worldwide meetups: http://duckduckgo.meetup.com/

Becoming a Host

Organizing a meetup group is a fun, rewarding experience and you don't need to be a DuckDuckHack expert or veteran developer to lead your own! (In fact, it's a great way to organize your first tech event, since you'll have our support and experience behind you.)

Shoot us an email with short answers to the following questions (so we know how to best help):

  • Where are you located? When are you thinking about hosting the event?
  • Experience (if any) personally creating or improving DuckDuckGo Instant Answers? Send us a link!
  • Experience (if any) hosting tech meetups

These questions will let us know how to support you as a meetup host.

Email them to meetup@duckduckgo.com - we should get back to you the same day.

Pick a Venue (and Your Favorite Pizza)

Great venues include:

  • Co-working spaces (they often love hosting open-source meetups).
  • Spacious tech offices (startups tend to jump at the chance to host).
  • Community locations: universities, maker spaces, and so on!

Food makes people happy, bond, and stay longer. Pizza is usually easiest and most agreeable option - we've got tons of tips we'll share on how to do this (including getting it sponsored).

Create an Event and Spread the Word

We'll help you think through:

  • Promoting your group and event.
  • Working with new members.
  • Communicating the group event timeline.

Our staff and Community Leaders have a ton of great advice to share from our experience - from how to increase attendance, to the best event tools, to free Uber vouchers.

We'll Totally Be There

Well, maybe not physically - but in every other way! We'll guide you at every step of creating your event, from planning the details, to promotion, to even having DuckDuckHack staff or community leader on live help standby during the event.

Grow

Our aim is for every group to meet at least once per month. We'll help you grow your group into a real force to be reckoned with in the open source community!
